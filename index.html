❓ Frequently Asked Questions
Most Colleen Hoover books are standalone and can be read in any order. For the best first experience, start with Verity (her most gripping), then It Ends with Us (her most beloved), then Ugly Love. The only series that must be read in order is the Slammed trilogy: Slammed → Point of Retreat → This Girl.
Within each series, yes — absolutely. ACOTAR, Throne of Glass, and Crescent City must each be read in order. But you can choose which series to start with. Most fans recommend starting with ACOTAR Book 1 or Throne of Glass Book 1. Save Crescent City for last as it contains crossover characters from all three series.
No — the Empyrean series by Rebecca Yarros must be read strictly in order. Each book ends on a cliffhanger and the next book picks up directly where it left off. Read: Fourth Wing → Iron Flame → Onyx Storm. Do not skip or read out of order.
The most recommended starting point is Mistborn: The Final Empire (Book 1). It's a complete, satisfying story that introduces Sanderson's world-building style without requiring any prior knowledge. If you prefer something shorter first, try The Emperor's Soul — a standalone novella. Avoid starting with The Way of Kings as it's over 1,000 pages and best enjoyed after you're already a fan.
Almost all John Grisham books are completely standalone — you can start anywhere. The only exception is his Jake Brigance series (A Time to Kill → A Time for Mercy → Sparring Partners) which should be read in order. Most readers recommend starting with The Firm or A Time to Kill as your introduction to Grisham.
